About Me

Aspiring Neuroscientist | Bibliophile | Writer | Mathlete

Where Biology Meets Computation

I am a 15-year-old explorer of the human mind, fascinated by the intersection of biology and logic. While most people see the brain as a mystery, I see it as the ultimate frontier. My journey started with a simple curiosity about how we think, which quickly evolved into a deep passion for Neuroscience. Whether I’m studying neuroplasticity or the mechanics of memory, I am driven by one goal: understanding the biological "code" that makes us who we are.

Beyond the lab, I am an avid reader and writer. I believe that literature is one of the best ways to observe the human condition that neuroscience seeks to explain. I spend my time diving into everything from classic literature to modern scientific journals, often documenting my findings through analytical writing. For me, writing isn’t just a hobby—it’s a way to map out complex ideas and share the wonders of the brain with others.

To keep my own "wetware" sharp, I practice mental math as a form of cognitive conditioning. I treat numerical calculations like a workout for my prefrontal cortex, constantly pushing the limits of my working memory and processing speed. This site is a collection of my research, my reading list, and my journey as I navigate the fascinating world of cognitive science. I'm always looking for new books to read or complex problems to solve!
